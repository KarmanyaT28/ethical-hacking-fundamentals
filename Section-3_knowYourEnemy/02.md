Hackers, Crackers and Cybercriminals


Hackers (often "white hats") ethically find vulnerabilities to improve security; Crackers ("black hats") maliciously break into systems for personal gain, data theft, or disruption, causing harm; and Cybercriminals is a broader term for anyone committing crimes online, often using hacking skills, encompassing crackers, identity thieves, and state-sponsored actors, focusing on financial fraud or espionage. 



Hackers (Ethical)
Definition: Security professionals who find and fix system weaknesses with permission.
Intent: To protect data, improve security, and test defenses.
Methods: Penetration testing, vulnerability assessments, ethical hacking.
Legality: Legal and often certified (e.g., CEH). 


Crackers (Malicious)
Definition: Individuals who illegally access systems for harmful purposes.
Intent: Personal gain, data theft, damage, or disruption (often called "black hats").
Methods: Phishing, malware, exploiting vulnerabilities, creating Trojans.
Legality: Illegal; often anonymous and lack certifications


Cybercriminals (Broad Category)
Definition: Anyone engaging in criminal activity online, from individuals to organized groups.
Intent: Wide-ranging, including financial fraud, espionage, terrorism.
Includes: Crackers, identity thieves, terrorists, and state-sponsored actors.
Scope: Encompasses all cybercrimes, affecting individuals, businesses, and governments. 



We have to beware of script kiddies

A script kiddie, also known as a skid, by definition, is an individual with little to no technical expertise using pre-existing automated tools or scripts to launch attacks on computer systems or networks.
