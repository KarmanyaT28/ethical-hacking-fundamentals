First Strategy - Avoidance Strategy
This is the best strategy for reducing risk related to giving out personal information

The avoidance strategy is simply avoiding using certain social media, not posting, not filling in forms, simply not registering, just not giving out that information and this deprives you of the advantages of the modern internet era and simply not possible in certain scenarios



Second Strategy - Compartmentalisation Strategy
You can use compartmentalisation strategy
This is having separate identities from each other and your real identity
This is a good way to separate social and real identities


Third Strategy - Content Strategy
Here you are only giving out carefully considered information against your real identity
But this strategy is risky as it inadvertently reveal information you didnt intent to release


Fourth Strategy - Audience Strategy
It is little bit more risky, it keeps your professional and personal network separate. By using Facebook for friends and family and Linkedin for your professional network, this can limit the exposure of your personal information but it is still out there

Do you know it
Can your adversary access it
Do you trust your audience with that information or viewed potentially by other audience

You configure your privacy settings to attempt to reduce the audience
